## Talk Summary

In many teams, test analytics dashboards are created to track quality metrics, automation coverage, or defect trends.  
However, when these numbers are not connected to actual decisions, they become nothing more than reporting.

This talk explores the difference between **reporting metrics** and **decision-driving analytics**.

The key idea is simple:  
If analytics does not influence what the team does next, it is not helping improve quality.

---

## Key Points

- Why most testing dashboards fail to influence decisions  
- The difference between **metrics visibility** and **actionable insights**  
- How teams can connect test analytics with product and release decisions  
- Practical ways testers can turn data into meaningful conversations

---

## Audience Takeaways

After this talk, participants will understand:

- Why analytics without decisions becomes noise  
- How to ask better questions using testing data  
- How testers can move from reporting metrics to influencing strategy
