*Ye Meri Ghazalein, Ye Meri Nazmein* by Ahmad Faraz is less like a traditional book and more like a collection of deeply felt emotions expressed through poetry. The book gathers some of Faraz's most powerful ghazals and nazms, reflecting themes of love, longing, separation, and quiet reflection.

Reading Faraz often feels personal. His poetry carries the weight of lived experience, yet it remains universal enough that almost anyone can find themselves somewhere within his verses. What I admire most is his ability to express complex emotions with elegance and simplicity.

One of my favorite ghazals from the book is:

रंग थे जो कभी चेहरे पे, उनका क्या हुआ।  
अब के हमने जो कहा, सुन के क्या हुआ ।।

In these lines, Faraz captures the fragile and temporary nature of emotions. Moments that once filled life with color slowly fade, leaving behind memories and unanswered questions. This bittersweet feeling runs through much of his poetry.

Each ghazal and nazm in this collection invites reflection. Some explore the pain of separation, others speak of identity, longing, or hope. The beauty of Faraz's poetry lies in how it quietly stays with you, making you pause and think long after you have finished reading.

For readers who enjoy thoughtful and introspective poetry, this collection is a treasure.
